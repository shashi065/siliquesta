## TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### ML Model

**Type:** Ensemble (XGBoost + Neural Network)

**XGBoost Component:**
- 3 independent models (delay, power, frequency)
- 240 estimators
- Max depth: 6
- Learning rate: 0.05
- Subsample: 0.9

**PyTorch Component:**
- Architecture: Input(15) → Linear(64) → ReLU → Dropout(0.12)
                       → Linear(64) → ReLU → Dropout(0.12)
                       → Linear(3)
- Training: 140 epochs, Adam optimizer (lr=0.001)
- Loss: MSELoss

**Ensemble Method:** Average predictions from both models

**Inputs (15 features):**
1. WN (NMOS width)
2. WP (PMOS width)
3. VDD (supply voltage)
4. Temp (temperature)
5. CL_FF (load capacitance)
6. Tech_node (technology)
7. Width_ratio (WP/WN)
8. Total_width (WN+WP)
9. Inv_tech (1/tech_node)
10-15. Process corners (SS, TT, FF, SF, FS, MC)

**Outputs (3 targets):**
1. Delay (ps)
2. Power (mW)
3. Frequency (GHz)

### Training

**Dataset:** 5,000 diverse circuit samples
- Generated from CMOS physics engine or ngspice
- Covers entire design space
- Balanced across corners and temperatures

**Validation Metrics:**
- Delay: R²=0.986, MAPE=3.2%
- Power: R²=0.972, MAPE=4.1%
- Frequency: R²=0.995, MAPE=2.1%
- Mean MAPE: 3.1%

### Optimization

**Algorithm:** Two-stage (global + local)

**Stage 1 - Global Search:**
- Method: Differential Evolution
- Iterations: 50
- Population: 15
- Mutation: (0.5, 1.5)
- Purpose: Escape local minima

**Stage 2 - Local Refinement:**
- Method: L-BFGS-B (quasi-Newton)
- Iterations: 50
- Purpose: Converge to optimum

**Multi-Objective Weights:**
- Performance: freq=0.5, power=0.2, delay=0.2, efficiency=0.1
- Power: freq=0.1, power=0.7, delay=0.1, efficiency=0.1
- Efficiency: freq=0.3, power=0.2, delay=0.1, efficiency=0.4
- Balanced: freq=0.3, power=0.3, delay=0.2, efficiency=0.2

### Confidence Calculation

**Components:**
1. Dropout Uncertainty (from PyTorch)
   - 12 forward passes with dropout enabled
   - Capture prediction variance
   
2. Model Disagreement
   - Gap between XGBoost and PyTorch
   - Indicates conflicting predictions
   
3. Normalization
   - Relative to feature scales
   - Produces confidence (0-1)

**Formula:**
```
uncertainty = mean(dropout_std + model_gap)
confidence = 1 - normalized_uncertainty
```

**Interpretation:**
- >0.95: Highly reliable
- 0.85-0.95: Good confidence
- 0.70-0.85: Moderate, consider validation
- <0.70: Low, validate with simulation
